## Findings

### Strengths

Annual policy review cycle established. Vulnerability management metrics tracked (scan coverage, remediation times). Incident lessons learned documented post-incident.

### Gaps

No structured continuous improvement program. Supplier lessons learned sessions not conducted. Operational metrics not systematically analyzed for trends. Policy updates do not consistently incorporate operational feedback.

## Score

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Actual Score | 2 |
| Target Score | 5 |

**Scoring rationale:** A 2 sits at the bottom edge of Some Security (2.0–4.9): improvement activity occurs, but ad hoc and by individual initiative, with no defined process. The evidence shows fragments — an annual policy review cycle, vulnerability management metrics (scan coverage, remediation times), and post-incident lessons learned — but no structured continuous improvement program stitches them together: metrics are not analyzed for trends, supplier lessons-learned sessions do not happen, and policy updates fail to consistently absorb operational feedback. Moving even to a 3 requires a documented improvement process; the Minimally Acceptable band (5.0) additionally demands that process run consistently across the operational scope with exceptions tracked.
