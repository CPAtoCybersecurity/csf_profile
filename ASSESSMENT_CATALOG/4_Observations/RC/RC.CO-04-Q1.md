**Assessor:** Steve Mercer, Internal Audit <steve.mercer@almasecurity.com>

**Observation Date:** 2026-03-16

**Testing Status:** Complete

## Testing Methods

| Method | Performed | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Examine | Yes | Reviewed breach notification procedures, public communication records from 2024 incidents, public trust score KPI data, incident response playbook public communication section |
| Interview | Yes | Interviewed leadership on public communication approval process, trust-rebuilding initiatives, and lessons learned from 2024 public communications |
| Test | No | Public communication procedures not independently tested; reviewed historical execution |

---

## Findings

### Strengths

- Breach notification procedures exist within the incident response playbook addressing regulatory notification requirements
- The 2024 security incidents triggered breach notification and public communication, providing real execution evidence
- Public trust score KPI tracking demonstrates organizational commitment to measuring and recovering public confidence
- Trust-rebuilding initiatives following 2024 incidents indicate lessons learned were applied to public communication strategy

### Gaps

- **No pre-approved messaging templates** — Public communication templates for incident recovery exist in draft form but have not been pre-approved for rapid deployment, creating delays during incidents
- **Approval workflow not formalized** — The public communication approval process (legal review, leadership sign-off) is understood but not documented as a formal workflow with defined roles and timelines
- **Breach notification compliance not independently verified** — Compliance with specific regulatory notification timelines and content requirements has not been independently validated
- **No public communication rehearsal** — Public messaging has not been tested through a tabletop or simulation exercise to validate the end-to-end approval and release process

---

## Score

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Actual Score | 3.5 |
| Target Score | 5 |

**Scoring rationale:** A 3.5 falls in the Some Security band (2.0–4.9), midway between the rubric's anchor 3 (defined but unreliable) and anchor 4 (regular execution with material weaknesses). Breach notification procedures exist in the playbook, the 2024 incidents demonstrated actual public communication under fire, and the public trust score KPI shows the outcome is being measured — but the messaging templates remain unapproved drafts, the legal-review and sign-off workflow is understood rather than documented, and regulatory notification compliance has never been independently verified. The Minimally Acceptable band (5.0) requires the process to execute consistently across its full scope with only minor flaws; until pre-approved templates and a formal approval workflow exist and have been exercised, rapid and compliant public communication depends on improvisation rather than a repeatable process.
