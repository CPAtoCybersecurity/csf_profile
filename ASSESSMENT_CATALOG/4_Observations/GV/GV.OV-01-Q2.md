## Findings

Cybersecurity strategy outcomes are reviewed quarterly and demonstrably drive resource allocation. The KPI framework the CISO instituted after the 2024 incidents now shows time-to-detect at 9 hours against the 81-hour October 2024 baseline, time-to-investigate at 14 hours, crown-jewel critical remediation (TTR-CJC) under 6 days, and all-systems critical remediation at 12 days as of this quarter's review. Detection-capability investments were approved by the board based directly on these KPI trends, and the strategy review minutes document the linkage between measured outcomes and each adjustment decision. The program's innovation-enabling posture — balancing risk strategies against operational needs — is stated in the strategy and observable in the approved investment mix.

### Strengths

- Board reviews strategy outcomes quarterly with documented linkage between KPI trends and investment decisions
- TTD improvement (81 hours → 9 hours) is tracked against the published January 2027 target of under 4 minutes
- Detection and response investments were approved on the strength of measured KPI trends, not anecdote
- Risk strategies are explicitly balanced against operational needs in the strategy review record

### Gaps

- TTR-CJC at just under 6 days remains far from the August 2027 target of under 16 hours; the glide path to target is not yet documented quarter-by-quarter
- Outcome reviews focus on detection/response KPIs; strategy outcomes for third-party risk (R6) and asset inventory (R4) are not yet represented on the dashboard

**Scoring rationale:** Score of 6 (Optimized) exceeds the board-approved target of 5: outcome review is periodic, evidenced, and demonstrably feeds strategy and investment decisions — the defining anchor of the Optimized band. The score does not reach Fully Optimized (7.0+) because the outcome dashboard covers only the detection/response KPI family; register risks R4 and R6 lack outcome measures, so strategy adjustment for those areas still relies on qualitative judgment. Holding at 6 from Q1 reflects consistent operation with no scope expansion.
